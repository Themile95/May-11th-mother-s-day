Happy Mother's Day To My Beloved Mother

Dear Mother

The Heart Of Home
From the moment I first opened my eyes,
You were there, my earth and skies.
A gentle voice, a steady hand,
My first true guide to understand.
You taught me love with soft goodnights,
And courage through the hardest fights.
You held me close when I was small,
And helped me rise from every fall.
Your days began before the sun,
And ended when your work was done—
But even then, you’d find the grace
To wear a smile upon your face.
You packed my lunch, tied my shoe,
Taught me how to think things through.
You calmed my storms and shared my joy,
Through every stage, your girl or boy.
The way you knew just what to say,
To lift my spirit, clear the way—
Your words were wisdom, calm and kind,
A treasure chest within my mind.
When I was lost or filled with doubt,
You’d wrap your arms and pull me out.
You saw in me what I could be,
Even when I couldn't see.
You gave me roots so I could grow,
And wings to see the world and go.
And though I’ve traveled far and wide,
I carry you here, deep inside.
You’re not just “Mom,” you’re soul and light,
A quiet force, a glowing knight.
You fought for me in silent ways,
In countless, selfless, unseen days.
The meals you made, the nights you stayed,
The dreams you chased but once delayed—
All for me, without regret,
A debt I never can forget.
No crown could match the worth you hold,
No poem catch your heart of gold.
Still I offer these humble lines,
To thank you for the endless times—
You gave your all, you stood so strong,
You knew the words when I was wrong,
You loved with fire, deep and true—
The world is better, Mom, through you.
So on this Mother’s Day I send
More love than I could ever lend.
You are my first, forever home—
My heart, my hero, all my own.
